# RECORD

---

## 1. Design Style

### Tone & Experience
- Quiet, slow, and observational; no urgency or failure states  
- Player interacts primarily through presence rather than control  
- Emotional centre built around stillness, gentle growth, and small changes  
- Mechanics should feel natural, unforced, and lightly responsive  

### Visual & Audio Direction
- Minimalist environment focused on a single tree  
- Soft, subtle motion (leaf drift, apple fall)  
- Audio cues tied to movement, presence, or time  
- No decorative effects unless they reinforce tone  

### Interaction Model
- One primary space; no scene switching  
- Interactions optional and non‑disruptive  
- The tree remains the focal point; player is secondary  
- System should support long‑form, passive engagement  

---

## 2. Development Process

### Repository Layout
- `game/` for scenes and logic  
- `assets/` for sprites, audio, and visual elements  
- `export/` for builds  
- `docs/` for internal notes and supporting files  

### Workflow Principles
- Slow, deliberate development pace  
- Tone and atmosphere take precedence over feature count  
- Early mechanics validated before expanding scope  
- All additions must preserve the quiet, observational nature of the project  

### Core Systems
- Leaf and apple fall patterns (timing, drift, weight)  
- Subtle visual shifts tied to time or presence  
- Lightweight event triggers for ambient behaviour  
- Deterministic behaviour unless randomness improves natural feel
