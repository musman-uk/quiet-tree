# Design Notes

These notes capture the early thinking behind *A Quiet Tree*.  
They are not final – they are seeds, ideas, and fragments that may grow or fall away.

## Themes

- stillness  
- gentle growth  
- quiet companionship  
- noticing small changes  
- the emotional weight of slow time  
- the comfort of simple, repeated actions  

The game should feel like spending time with something living, without needing to control it.

## Core Scope

The first version of *A Quiet Tree* is intentionally small.  
One tree, one space, one mechanic.

- leaves fall slowly and naturally  
- apples fall occasionally, with a little more weight  
- the player can interact only by being present and responding to what the tree offers  

This limited scope keeps the project focused and allows the tone to lead the design.

## Early Mechanics (Exploratory)

- falling leaves that drift in gentle patterns  
- apples that drop at a slower, more deliberate rhythm  
- soft audio cues tied to movement, presence, or time  
- subtle visual shifts in the tree as days pass  
- a sense of quiet reward in simply watching things unfold  

Nothing here is complex. The mechanics should feel like weather – small, calm, and unforced.

## Future Additions (Possibilities)

These are ideas for later, once the core is stable:

- a basket to catch apples  
- small messages or reflections revealed through interaction  
- seasonal variations or colour shifts  
- tiny creatures or ambient details that appear over time  
- a sense of companionship that grows quietly, without dialogue  

These are not promises – only branches the project may grow into.

## Tone & Interaction

- no urgency, no failure states  
- interactions should feel optional, never demanded  
- the tree is the centre, not the player  
- the world should feel soft, slow, and lightly responsive  

The design will emerge slowly, shaped by what feels true to the project’s tone.
